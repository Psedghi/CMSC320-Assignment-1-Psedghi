Q3: What is the difference between merging and rebasing?

Answer: Merging combines branches and preserves the full commit history while rebasing rewrites history to create a linear sequence of 
commits, making the commit history clear but more complex if there are conflicts. Merging is perfered if we want to keep a full history of 
changes while rebasing is for maintaining a clean commit history.
